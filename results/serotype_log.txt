--- INTERPRETATION GUIDE ---

CAUSAL HYPOTHESIS:
  Entropy rises (serotype co-circulation)
    -> new serotype encounters susceptible population
    -> Rt increases (f_residual > 0)
    -> cases surge

If this causal chain holds, the CCF should show:
  - Negative CCF lag: entropy/turnover LEADS Rt (serotype change precedes Rt rise)
  - Post-switch P(elevated) >> 50%: Rt rises AFTER serotype switches

EARLY WARNING SIGNALS:
  1. ENTROPY as early warning for Rt: if entropy rises before Rt,
     monitoring serotype diversity could predict Rt surges.
  2. Rt as early warning for CASES: since Rt increases precede case
     surges by definition (renewal equation), detecting Rt anomalies
     (f_residual > 0) provides lead time for case increases.

The two-stage early warning chain:
  Entropy/serotype monitoring -> Rt anomaly detection -> case surge prediction

This analysis is descriptive and hypothesis-generating. With ~3-4 major
switches in 10 years, there is no sample size for formal testing.
